### General rules (apply to all question types)

- Do NOT invent facts.
- Keep language age-appropriate.
- Hints must not reveal the answer verbatim. 
- Hints must not include any word, phrase, or identifier that would be accepted as a correct answer if typed by the learner. This includes canonical answers, aliases, titles, identities, exact terms, or uniquely identifying phrases.
- Hint 3 may describe attributes, roles, effects, or context, but must avoid uniquely identifying the answer on its own.
- **For Numeric/Date answers (The Funnel Rule): You must strictly order hints from broad to specific. Do NOT place the most specific mathematical or chronological clue in Hint 1 or 2.**
  - **Hint 1 (Magnitude/Era): Establish the broad scale or century (e.g., "It is more than 5 but less than 15" or "It happened in the late 1400s").**
  - **Hint 2 (Contextual Bound): Relate the number to a known lore event or provide a tighter mathematical bound (e.g., "It is a prime number" or "It occurred a few years before the first Triwizard Tournament").**
  - **Hint 3 (Proximity): Bring the user right to the edge without giving the exact digits (e.g., "It is exactly one less than the number of players on a Quidditch team").**
- The explanation must justify why the answer is correct (2–4 sentences). 
- Answer variations must be semantically equivalent to the canonical answer.
- Answer variations should represent the answer itself, not its justification.

### Question-type–specific guidance

#### FR (Free Response)
- Answer variations should capture reasonable ways a learner might express the correct answer.
- Capture full names, common surnames, and established titles (e.g., "Professor Snape").
- Avoid drifting into full explanations or new facts.

#### EX (Explanatory) 
- You must provide exactly THREE answer_variations following this "Multi-Resolution" structure:
  1. Index 0 (Full Direct): A full, direct explanation (Max 25 words).
  2. Index 1 (Core Logic): A stripped-down version containing only the essential entity and causal logic (Max 12 words).
  3. Index 2 (Key Terms): A comma-separated list of the essential proper nouns and verbs required for a 'correct' mark.
  4. Strict Rule: Do NOT include labels such as "[Comprehensive]" or "[Semantic Core]" in the strings. Return the plain text only.
- Hint 3 should surface the conceptual lens (theme, motive, character insight) without stating the resolved conclusion.

#### MCQ (Multiple Choice)
- Generate hints/explanation as if the learner does NOT see the correct option highlighted.
- Hints should guide reasoning toward the correct option, not eliminate options mechanically.
- Answer variations may include partial keywords from the correct option that uniquely identify it among the choices.
- Do NOT include distractor options in variations.

### Answer-variation logic by Implicit Form
Identify the "form" of the answer before generating variations:
1. Entity: Focus on name ordering, aliases, and titles (e.g., "Lord Voldemort", "You-Know-Who").
2. Numeric: Focus on digits and number words.
3. Action: Describe the physical or magical act concisely.
4. Date: Represent the date at the precision requested in the question.
