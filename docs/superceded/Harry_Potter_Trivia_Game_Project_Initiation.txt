
Project Title: Harry Potter Trivia Game

1. **Target Audience**:
   - Primary audience: Children 10+ and Harry Potter fans.
   - The trivia questions will be randomly chosen from a dataset. Later versions may include difficulty levels.

2. **Gameplay**:
   - Linear gameplay with a starting screen where players input their name and pick their house.
   - The story revolves around representing the trivia team for their house during the "Second Wizarding War," the "Battle of Hogwarts," and featuring their favorite Auror, Harry Potter, who later becomes the Minister of Magic.
   - Incorrect answers do not earn points, and players will receive a score out of the total number of questions at the end.
   - There will be winner labels such as "Newbie Wizard" and "Harry Potter Expert" based on the final score.
   - Answers will be typed in by players. There will be a mechanism to handle spelling mistakes in answers (e.g., case-insensitive comparison).
   - Minimal animations will be used, with visuals such as house images, Harry Potter-themed backgrounds, and audio effects for certain events (e.g., correct or incorrect answers).
   - The game will be deployed online for easy access and sharing with friends.

3. **Tech Stack**:
   - The game will be built using **Streamlit**, a simple and fast framework to create interactive apps with Python.
   - **Streamlit Features** to Use:
     - **st.image()**: Display images (e.g., house crests, character images, Hogwarts backgrounds).
     - **st.audio()**: Play sound effects (e.g., spell-casting, correct/incorrect answers).
     - **st.text_input()**: Allow players to type their answers.
     - **st.success() / st.error()**: Show feedback for correct or incorrect answers.
     - **st.radio()**: Let players pick their house.
     - **st.write()**: Show game instructions, scores, and progress.

4. **Deployment**:
   - The game will be deployed on **Streamlit Cloud**, which allows easy sharing via a URL. The game will be hosted online for friends and fans to play by simply visiting the URL.

5. **Features to Implement**:
   - Player Input: Name entry and house selection.
   - Question Display: Show one question at a time with answer input and validation.
   - Scoring: Track correct answers and show the final score at the end.
   - Randomize Questions: Load questions from the Hugging Face dataset and shuffle them for each playthrough.
   - Visuals: Display house images, Hogwarts-themed backgrounds, and magical elements.
   - Sound Effects: Play sound effects based on player actions (e.g., correct answers).
   - Winner Labels: Assign labels based on score to motivate players (e.g., "Harry Potter Expert" for top scorers).
   
6. **Milestones**:
   - **Week 1**: Learn Streamlit basics, build player input, and create a basic game flow with one question and feedback.
   - **Week 2**: Integrate question randomization from the dataset, add scoring, and implement the game completion screen.
   - **Week 3**: Add visuals (house images, Hogwarts backgrounds) and sound effects (correct/incorrect answer sounds).
   - **Week 4**: Polish the game, test the gameplay, and deploy the game on Streamlit Cloud.

7. **Challenges**:
   - Ensuring that the answer validation is forgiving of small spelling mistakes.
   - Making sure that visuals and sounds are well-integrated without affecting the game’s performance.
