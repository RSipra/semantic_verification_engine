# ADR-P2-004: Generative Model Selection (Gemini)

**Status:** ✅ Accepted  
**Phase:** Phase 2 (System & Semantic Intelligence)

## Decision

Standardize on the Google Gemini API for synthetic question generation in the Content Factory.

## Options considered

1. **Google Gemini (Flash / Pro)**
2. **OpenAI (GPT series)**
3. **DeepSeek (Reasoner models via direct or third-party access)**

## Selected option

Option 1 (Google Gemini)

## Rationale

The model selection was guided by practical constraints rather than model capability alone. Synthetic question generation is a **batch, offline workload** with well-defined prompts and quality gates, making cost, rate limits, and operational friction the dominant factors.

Google Gemini was selected because:

- **Viable free-tier access**  
  Gemini models offer generous free-tier rate limits sufficient to generate the full dataset within a reasonable time window, enabling experimentation and batch runs at zero cost.

- **Predictable and low paid-tier cost**  
  Even in a worst-case scenario requiring paid usage, total generation cost remains well below the project’s budget cap, making Gemini a low-risk choice.

- **Lower operational friction**  
  OpenAI no longer provides a free tier, and DeepSeek requires either prepaid credits or integration via third-party platforms, adding unnecessary complexity for a one-time, well-scoped batch task.

- **Ecosystem alignment**  
  Native integration with the GCP stack simplifies authentication, logging, and experimentation.

A detailed token, rate-limit, and cost analysis supporting this decision is documented separately.

## Trade-offs

**Pros:**
- Enables full dataset generation within free-tier limits
- Low and predictable cost if paid usage is required
- Minimal setup and operational overhead

**Cons:**
- Model choice is driven by practicality rather than absolute quality
- Advanced reasoning models are deprioritized in favor of throughput and cost control

## Risks

- Model quality may vary across question types, requiring selective use of higher-tier models
- Rate limits may change over time

## Consequences & Future considerations

- Model selection can be revisited if quality requirements increase
- Prompts and datasets are portable and not tightly coupled to Gemini
- Multi-provider generation remains possible if constraints change
